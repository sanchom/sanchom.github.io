Development plans, zoning, and public hearings in Vancouver

Vancouver has sevбґerбґal official deбґvelбґopбґment plans (ODPs). These plans limбґit the uses that are alбґlowed in cerбґtain arбґeas. Zoning and public hearings

Municipalities use zonбґing byбґlaws to conбґtrol land use. The Court of Appeal for British Columbia has said:

[44] Б─і There is an abunбґdance of juбґrispruбґdence on the funбґdaбґmenбґtal imбґporбґtance of noбґtice to the leбґgitбґiбґmaбґcy of the deбґciбґsion-makбґing process in land use and zonбґing casбґes. [45] A pubбґlic hearбґing on land use and zonбґing byбґlaws serves at least two imбґporбґtant funcбґtions: it proбґvides an opбґporбґtuбґniбґty for those whose inбґterбґests might be affected by such a deбґciбґsion to make their views known to the deбґciбґsion-makбґer and it gives the deбґciбґsion-makбґer the benefit of pubбґlic exбґamбґiбґnaбґtion and disбґcusбґsion of the isбґsues surбґroundбґing the adopбґtion or reбґjecбґtion of the proбґposed byбґlaw.

[46] Procedures aimed at enбґsurбґing a minбґiбґmum stanбґdard of raбґtioбґnalбґiбґty in the deбґciбґsion-makбґing process are more likeбґly to enбґhance the qualбґiбґty of the deбґciбґsion and the pubбґlicБ─≥s acбґcepбґtance of it than deбґciбґsions based on undisбґclosed inбґforбґmaбґtion, or on inбґcomбґplete or ill-conбґsidбґered facts.

[47] As well, parбґticбґiбґpaбґtoбґry proбґceбґdures such as pubбґlic hearбґings on land use or zonбґing byбґlaws tend to disбґpel perбґcepбґtions of arбґbiбґtrariбґness, bias or othбґer imбґproбґpriбґety on the part of loбґcal govбґernбґment in the deбґciбґsion-makбґing process and tend to enбґhance pubбґlic acбґcepбґtance of such deбґciбґsions. Put anбґothбґer way, the perбґcepбґtion, if not the fact, of arбґbiбґtrariбґness or bias is more likeбґly to arise if the duty to enбґsure proбґceбґdurбґal fairбґness is not obбґserved.

VancouverБ─≥s Procedure Bylaw deбґscribes the process that Vancouver Council has adoptбґed for pubбґlic hearбґings. It is rightбґly onerбґous and time-conбґsumбґing.

Official community/development plans

Official comбґmuбґniбґty plans and official deбґvelбґopбґment plans work at a levбґel above zonбґing.

All loбґcal govбґernбґments othбґer than Vancouver have a tool availбґable to them called an official comбґmuбґniбґty plan (OCP). Official comбґmuбґniбґty plans canбґnot be adoptбґed withбґout a pubбґlic hearбґing. Once a counбґcil adopts an OCP, counбґcil beбґcomes conбґstrained by the OCP. VancouverБ─≥s official deбґvelбґopбґment plans simбґiбґlarбґly conбґstrain Council. However, there are two maбґjor differences. Again, the Local Government Act (apбґplyбґing to all muбґnicбґiбґpalбґiбґties othбґer than Vancouver) says that Б─°[a] loбґcal govбґernбґment may waive the holdбґing of a pubбґlic hearбґing on a proбґposed zonбґing byбґlaw if (a) an official comбґmuбґniбґty plan is in effect for the area that is subбґject to the byбґlaw, and (b) the byбґlaw is conбґsisбґtent with the official comбґmuбґniбґty plan.Б─² There just is no equivбґaбґlent in the Vancouver Charter.

Why is Vancouver different?

Vancouver didnБ─≥t start out so different.

As reбґcentбґly as 1979, all muбґnicбґiбґpalбґiбґties in British Columbia were in roughбґly the same poбґsiбґtion that Vancouver is in toбґday on this isбґsue. The imбґpeбґtus for the 1985 amendбґment was the douбґble-hearбґing reбґquireбґment that had exбґistбґed unбґder the Municipal Act and which apбґplied to muбґnicбґiбґpalбґiбґties othбґer than Vancouver. In the earбґly 1980s, the comбґplaint was that two stages of pubбґlic hearбґing was a Б─°duбґpliбґcaбґtion of effortБ─² and that planбґning Б─°had a tenбґdenбґcy to creбґate unбґnecбґesбґsary buбґreauбґcraбґcy.Б─² Vancouver is facбґing simбґiбґlar isбґsues toбґday. If Vancouver Council is goбґing to use a pubбґlic hearбґing process for its ODPs, it should get the same efficiency reбґward that othбґer muбґnicбґiбґpalбґiбґties get (the abilбґiбґty to waive the pubбґlic hearбґing for reбґzonбґings conбґsisбґtent with the ODP).
